DOCUMENT TYPE: Knowledge Base Reference
TOPIC: Key Questions to Ask Before Signing Any Employment Document
APPLICABLE TO: offer_letter, employment_contract, internship_agreement, nda

---

OVERVIEW

This document provides a structured list of questions to ask employers, hiring managers, or HR before signing any employment-related document. These are the questions that reveal whether an offer is as good as it appears on paper.

---

QUESTIONS ABOUT COMPENSATION

1. Can you provide a full CTC breakup showing each component?
Why: Identifies inflated CTC with non-cash benefits, low Basic Salary, or excessive variable pay.

2. What percentage of the variable pay is actually paid on average?
Why: Variable pay is often described as "up to 20%" but historically paid at 60–80% of target. Get the last 3-year average payout percentage.

3. What are the performance metrics that determine variable payout?
Why: "At management discretion" variables are worthless. Look for measurable, individual KPIs.

4. What is the appraisal cycle and when was the last increment batch?
Why: Joining just after an increment batch means waiting 12+ months for your first raise.

5. Is the joining bonus clawback only on voluntary resignation, or also on company-initiated termination?
Why: Some companies claw back the bonus even if they let you go.

6. What is the average increment percentage in the last 3 years?
Why: Reveals whether the company is keeping up with market salaries.

7. Are ESOPs included in CTC? What is the vesting schedule?
Why: ESOPs included in CTC are not liquid. Understand the actual cash vs equity split.

---

QUESTIONS ABOUT ROLE AND GROWTH

8. What does the first 90 days on the job look like?
Why: A well-run organization has a clear onboarding plan. "Figure it out" is a red flag.

9. Who will I report to, and what is their management style?
Why: Your manager is the #1 factor in your job satisfaction. Meet them before accepting.

10. What does the career ladder look like for this role?
Why: Is there a clear path to the next level? What is the average time to promotion?

11. What happened to the person who previously held this role?
Why: If they left, why? If promoted, where did they go? If the role is new, why?

12. What are the team's biggest challenges right now?
Why: Reveals real work environment, team dynamics, and whether the role will be impactful or administrative.

13. What tech stack will I be working on? Is it modern or legacy?
Why: Legacy tech debt limits your learning and marketability for future opportunities.

---

QUESTIONS ABOUT WORK-LIFE AND CULTURE

14. What are the expected working hours? Are weekends common?
Why: "We work hard" can mean anything. Ask specifically whether nights and weekends are common.

15. How does the team handle on-call or production incidents?
Why: For SDE/backend roles, oncall rotation can significantly affect quality of life.

16. What is the remote/hybrid policy? Is it subject to change?
Why: "Currently hybrid" can become "mandatory in-office" — get the policy in writing.

17. What is the attrition rate in the team / company?
Why: High attrition = burned out team or poor management. Industry benchmark: 10–15% for IT services; 8–12% for product companies.

18. What percentage of interns receive PPO? (For internship agreements)
Why: Below 50% conversion indicates the internship program is not structured for real hiring.

---

QUESTIONS ABOUT THE OFFER TERMS

19. Is the non-compete clause enforceable, and what does the company expect from it?
Why: Most Indian companies know it's unenforceable but include it anyway. Asking this question reveals their intent.

20. What is the notice period on the company's side? Is it equal to mine?
Why: Asymmetric notice periods are a red flag (e.g., 3-month employee notice, 15-day company notice).

21. Can I buy out the notice period? Who approves it?
Why: Some companies allow buyout only at management discretion — which means you may be held for the full duration.

22. What is the probation period? How and when is confirmation communicated?
Why: Without written confirmation, probation can extend indefinitely.

23. Does the employment bond apply from day 1? What triggers it?
Why: Some bonds kick in before you've even completed training.

24. Can I take up open-source contributions or freelance work outside office hours?
Why: Broad exclusivity clauses can restrict this. Know the limits before you start.

---

QUESTIONS ABOUT BENEFITS AND INSURANCE

25. What is the sum insured under the group health insurance? Does it cover family?
Why: ₹2 lakh coverage is inadequate; ₹5 lakh+ floater is the current standard.

26. What happens to health insurance coverage during the notice period?
Why: Some companies deactivate insurance coverage when you resign. Verify.

27. Is the insurance premium included in CTC?
Why: If yes, it inflates CTC without giving you cash.

28. What is the PF calculation basis? Is it capped at ₹15,000 Basic or on actual Basic?
Why: Capped PF reduces your long-term retirement corpus while inflating take-home marginally.

29. What is the leave encashment policy on exit?
Why: Earned leave should be encashed. Some companies cap the encashable days.

---

QUESTIONS ABOUT FINANCIAL HEALTH (STARTUP SPECIFIC)

30. What is the company's runway? When was the last funding round?
Why: A startup with 6 months of runway and no fundraise in progress is a red flag.

31. What is the company's revenue and growth rate?
Why: Profitability and growth trend reveal sustainability.

32. Have there been any layoffs or pay cuts in the last 2 years?
Why: History of financial distress is a leading indicator.

33. What is the investor composition? Do investors have board control?
Why: Certain investor boards are known for aggressive cost-cutting that affects employees.

34. What is the company's policy on salary payments during funding gaps?
Why: Early-stage startups may defer salaries during cash crunches. Know this upfront.

---

QUESTIONS FOR NDA CONTEXT (VENDOR / PARTNER / CONSULTING)

35. What specific information is considered confidential under this NDA?
Why: Ensures you understand the scope before signing.

36. Can I use general learnings and skills from this engagement in future work?
Why: NDAs should not restrict you from using knowledge and skills — only specific confidential data.

37. How long will my obligation to keep information confidential last?
Why: Indefinite NDAs for general business information are excessive.

38. If I need to comply with a court order or regulatory request, am I protected from breach claims?
Why: Ensures you won't be sued for doing what the law requires.

---

HOW TO ASK THESE QUESTIONS WITHOUT SEEMING DIFFICULT

Frame questions as due diligence, not distrust:
"I'm very excited about this opportunity. I'm doing my research to make sure I understand the full picture before I sign."

Ask in email (not verbally only):
This creates a record. Verbal promises are difficult to enforce; written responses are better.

Ask through HR, not the hiring manager:
HR expects these questions. Hiring managers sometimes take them personally.

Accept that some questions won't be answered:
Financial details (revenue, runway) at private companies may not be shared. This is normal.

The right company will welcome these questions:
A company that reacts negatively to thoughtful questions about compensation and terms is revealing its culture.
