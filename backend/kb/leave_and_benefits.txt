OVERVIEW

Leave entitlements and benefits are often glossed over when evaluating an offer, but they significantly affect work-life quality and total compensation value. This document covers statutory requirements and market benchmarks.

---

STATUTORY LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS (INDIA)

EARNED LEAVE / PRIVILEGE LEAVE (PL/EL):
Mandated by: Factories Act, Shops and Establishments Acts (state-wise)
Minimum: 1 day per 20 days worked (approximately 15 days/year for full-time employees)
Standard market practice: 15–21 days/year
Best practice: 24–30 days/year (more common in MNCs and product companies)
Red flag: Less than 12 earned leave days per year for a full-time role
Carry-forward: Most companies allow carry-forward of 30–60 days; encash unused leave at year-end or on exit.

SICK LEAVE (SL / MEDICAL LEAVE):
Mandated: Most state Shops Acts mandate 12 sick days/year
Standard: 12 sick days, separate from earned leave
Best practice: Sick leave not deducted for short illnesses (trust-based for 1–2 days)
Red flag: Sick leave deducted from earned leave balance — merges two different entitlements.

CASUAL LEAVE (CL):
Standard: 7–12 casual leave days/year
Note: Casual leave is typically not encashable; it lapses at year-end.
Red flag: No casual leave separate from earned leave.

MATERNITY LEAVE:
Mandated by: Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (as amended 2017)
Entitlement: 26 weeks paid maternity leave for the first two children; 12 weeks for the third child onwards
Eligibility: Must have worked for 80 days in the 12 months before expected delivery
Red flag: Maternity leave less than 26 weeks for first/second child — violation of law.
Additional: Crèche facility mandated for establishments with 50+ employees.

PATERNITY LEAVE:
Statutory requirement: NO statutory paternity leave for private sector employees in India (as of 2024)
Standard market practice: 5–15 days
Best practice (MNCs): 4–12 weeks paternity leave
Red flag: Paternity leave of 0–2 days is below market norm for tech companies.

PARENTAL LEAVE (NON-BIRTH PARENT):
Emerging practice: Some companies extend maternity leave benefits to non-birth parents and adoptive parents.
Best practice: Equal leave for all parents regardless of gender or birth vs adoption.

MARKET BENCHMARKS FOR LEAVE (TECH SECTOR, INDIA 2024)

FAANG/Product companies (Google, Microsoft, Meta):
- Earned leave: 20–25 days
- Sick leave: Unlimited (trust-based) or 12 days
- Paternity: 4–12 weeks
- Bereavement: 3–20 days depending on relationship

Large IT services (TCS, Infosys, Wipro):
- Earned leave: 18–21 days
- Sick leave: 12 days
- Paternity: 5 days
- Holidays: 10–12 public holidays + 4–5 restricted holidays

Startups:
- Earned leave: 15–24 days (sometimes unlimited PTO at Series B+)
- Sick leave: Often trust-based (no formal limit)
- Paternity: 1–4 weeks
- "Unlimited PTO": Be cautious — in practice, unlimited PTO can lead to employees taking LESS leave due to social pressure.

---

HOLIDAY POLICY

National holidays in India: 3 mandated (Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti)
Festival holidays: State-specific (Diwali, Dussehra, Eid, Pongal, etc.)
Standard: 10–12 public holidays + 3–5 restricted/optional holidays
Restricted holidays: Employee can choose 2–3 from a list of ~10 regional festivals
Red flag: Less than 10 total holidays
Red flag: No provision for state-specific holidays for employees in different states.

HEALTH INSURANCE STANDARDS

MANDATED (ESI — EMPLOYEES STATE INSURANCE):
Applies to: Employees earning ≤ ₹21,000/month
Contribution: 3.25% employer + 0.75% employee
Provides: Medical, maternity, disability, and dependent benefits through ESI hospitals

MARKET STANDARD (ABOVE ESI THRESHOLD — IT SECTOR):
Group health insurance:
- Standard coverage: ₹3–5 lakh sum insured per employee
- Best practice: ₹5 lakh floater (employee + spouse + children)
- FAANG/large MNCs: ₹10 lakh+ family floater
- Dental and vision: Not standard in India; increasingly offered at large companies

Top-up plans: Some companies offer optional top-up coverage at subsidized group rates.
Red flag: No health insurance for a role where ESI does not apply (salary > ₹21,000/month)
Red flag: Health insurance premium included in CTC (taxed as perquisite for premiums above ₹50,000/year)

TERM LIFE INSURANCE:
Standard: 2–4x annual CTC term life coverage in group policy.
Best practice: 5x annual CTC
Red flag: No group term life insurance

ACCIDENT INSURANCE (EDLI/PERSONAL ACCIDENT):
EDLI: Mandatory under EPFO — provides ₹7 lakh death benefit to PF account holders
Personal accident: Often provided separately, standard ₹25 lakh–₹1 crore coverage

OTHER BENEFITS (MARKET STANDARD)

FOOD/MEAL:
- Office cafeteria subsidy: Common in large campuses
- Meal allowance: ₹2,200/month tax-exempt component (Sodexo/meal vouchers)

TRANSPORT:
- Company cab or transport allowance: ₹1,600–₹3,200/month (not fully tax-exempt under new regime)
- Some companies offer free cabs for night shifts

WELLNESS:
- Gym reimbursement: ₹3,000–₹10,000/year
- Mental health support (EAP programs): Increasingly standard post-2020
- Employee assistance programs with counseling sessions

LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT:
Standard: ₹25,000–₹1,00,000/year L&D budget per employee
Best practice: Coursera/Udemy/conference attendance covered
Important: Is this a reimbursement (taxable) or direct payment by company (non-taxable)?

PHONE/INTERNET:
Standard: ₹500–₹2,000/month phone reimbursement
Remote roles: ₹1,000–₹2,000/month internet reimbursement

---

LEAVE ENCASHMENT AND F&F

Leave encashment on resignation:
Standard: Earned/privilege leave accumulated but not taken is paid out in F&F settlement.
Formula: (Monthly Basic / 26) × number of leave days outstanding
Red flag: Contract states earned leave lapses on resignation (not encashed)

Leave encashment taxation:
Exempt up to ₹3 lakh for private sector employees on retirement/resignation (increased from ₹3 lakh in 2023 Budget to ₹25 lakh — verify current limit).
