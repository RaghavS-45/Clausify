PROBATION PERIOD STANDARDS

A probation period is a trial phase where the employer assesses the employee's performance and fit before confirming full employment. During probation, termination is typically easier for the employer.

STANDARD PROBATION DURATIONS:
- Fresher/Entry level: 3 months (most common)
- Lateral hire (junior): 3–6 months
- Mid/senior hire: 6 months
- Executive hire: 3–6 months with defined KPIs
- Red flag: Probation exceeding 6 months for non-executive roles
- Red flag: No stated end date for probation ("probation until satisfactory performance")

WHAT TYPICALLY CHANGES DURING PROBATION:
- Notice period is shorter (7–30 days vs 60–90 days post-confirmation)
- Annual leave entitlement may be prorated or restricted
- Company may not contribute to gratuity until post-confirmation (note: gratuity by law starts from day one)
- Employee may not be eligible for performance bonus during probation
- ESOP grants typically begin vesting only after probation

PROBATION EXTENSION:
Standard: Company can extend probation once for a defined period (usually 1–3 months) with written notice.
Red flag: Contract allows unlimited probation extensions with no cap.
Red flag: No written communication required for probation extension.

CONFIRMATION:
Standard: Confirmation should be in writing, triggered automatically at end of probation unless the employee is notified of extension or termination.
Red flag: Confirmation is at "management's discretion" with no timeline specified — allows indefinite probation.
Best practice: Contract should state "employment is deemed confirmed if no written extension is issued within 7 days of probation end date."

TERMINATION CLAUSE STANDARDS

Termination clauses define how and when employment can be ended by either party.

TYPES OF TERMINATION:

1. TERMINATION WITH NOTICE (Without Cause):
Standard: Company can terminate for business reasons (restructuring, performance, budget) by giving the contractual notice period.
Standard notice from company side: Equal to employee's notice period (symmetry)
Red flag: Company notice period shorter than employee's notice period
Red flag: Company can terminate "at will" without any notice

2. TERMINATION FOR CAUSE (Gross Misconduct):
Standard: Immediate termination without notice is permitted only for serious misconduct: fraud, theft, harassment, criminal activity, disclosure of confidential information.
Red flag: "Cause" defined so broadly it includes minor performance issues.
Red flag: No due process or inquiry procedure described before dismissal for cause.

3. TERMINATION DURING PROBATION:
Standard: Shorter notice (7–14 days) or immediate termination during probation period.
Acceptable: One week's notice or pay in lieu during probation.

4. TERMINATION BY MUTUAL CONSENT:
Standard: Both parties agree to end employment. No notice required from either side.
Common in: Startup exits, senior leadership transitions.

SEVERANCE AND F&F (FULL AND FINAL SETTLEMENT)

Standard F&F timeline: 30–45 days after last working day for most Indian companies.
Red flag: F&F delayed beyond 60 days.
Red flag: F&F contingent on no claims or complaints (gag clause).

COMPONENTS OF F&F SETTLEMENT:
- Salary for days worked in final month
- Leave encashment (earned/privilege leave balance)
- Variable pay (prorated if applicable per contract)
- Reimbursements pending
- Gratuity (if >5 years of service)
- PF transfer/withdrawal
- Release of relieving letter and experience letter

Red flag: Relieving letter withheld pending any dispute — this is coercive and may constitute wrongful withholding.
Red flag: Experience letter withheld as leverage — same issue.

---

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)

A PIP is a document placing an employee on structured performance review, typically as a precursor to termination.

Standard PIP duration: 30–90 days
Standard: PIP should include clear, measurable, achievable targets.
Red flag: PIP with unrealistic targets (a "sham PIP" designed to enable termination)
Red flag: PIP issued during maternity leave, medical leave, or right after a complaint/grievance
Red flag: No right of appeal or HR review built into PIP process

Legal note: Terminating an employee for poor performance after a proper PIP process is generally upheld. Terminating for performance without a PIP is more legally risky for the employer and is sometimes used as grounds for wrongful termination claims.

WRONGFUL TERMINATION IN INDIA

India does not have a general "wrongful termination" statute for white-collar employees (unlike the US). However, employees can challenge termination under:

1. Terms of the employment contract (breach of notice period, unauthorized cause)
2. Industrial Disputes Act (for employees earning below ₹1.6 lakh/month — includes requirement for prior government approval for retrenchment in factories with 100+ workers)
3. Constitutional protections (for government/PSU employees)
4. Discrimination law (termination based on gender, pregnancy, disability, caste — actionable)

Practical reality: Most white-collar IT employees do not pursue legal action even when terminated improperly, due to cost, time, and the social stigma of litigation. Companies rely on this to manage exits cheaply.

TERMINATION DUE TO ACQUISITION OR RESTRUCTURING

Standard: If a company is acquired, your employment contract should transfer to the acquirer under the same terms ("Transfer of undertaking" principle).
Red flag: Contract says "employment is automatically terminated upon change of control" — this strips protection in acquisitions.
Standard: Retention bonuses are common in acquisitions; they typically vest over 12–24 months with cliff periods.

CHECKLIST: WHAT TO LOOK FOR IN TERMINATION CLAUSES

- [ ] Is the notice period from the company equal to yours?
- [ ] Is "cause" for immediate termination narrowly and clearly defined?
- [ ] Is there a due process / inquiry procedure before termination for cause?
- [ ] What is the notice period during probation?
- [ ] Is F&F timeline defined (ideally ≤30 days)?
- [ ] Is relieving letter/experience letter explicitly committed to in the contract?
- [ ] Is there a PIP requirement before performance-based termination?
- [ ] What happens to ESOP/unvested equity on termination?
- [ ] Is there any payment on termination (severance)?
